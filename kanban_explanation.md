# Kanban Board Explanation

A Kanban board is a visual project management tool used to track tasks across different stages of a workflow. It uses columns such as "To Do," "In Progress," and "Done" to represent the progress of tasks.

---

## How the Board Visualizes Workflow

The Kanban board created for this project represents each stage of development:

- Backlog: All identified tasks that are not yet prioritized
- To Do: Tasks selected for the current sprint
- In Progress: Tasks currently being worked on
- Testing: Tasks being validated to ensure they meet requirements
- Blocked: Tasks that cannot proceed due to dependencies or issues
- In Review: Tasks being reviewed before completion
- Done: Completed tasks

This structure provides clear visibility of task progress and allows quick identification of the current state of work.

---

## Work-In-Progress (WIP)

To avoid overload and improve focus, the board limits work-in-progress by ensuring only a few tasks are in the "In Progress" column at a time. This prevents bottlenecks and ensures that tasks are completed before new ones are started.

---

## Support for Agile Principles

The Kanban board supports Agile principles by:

- Enabling continuous delivery of features
- Allowing flexibility to adapt to changes
- Providing transparency of task progress
- Supporting incremental development

The addition of columns such as "Testing" and "Blocked" improves workflow management by ensuring quality assurance and identifying delays early.

Overall, the board helps manage tasks efficiently while aligning with Agile development practices.
